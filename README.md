<h2>
<img src="https://img.icons8.com/color/28/error.png"/>
Problem Understanding
</h2>

<p>
Gig workers such as food delivery partners depend completely on daily earnings. Environmental disruptions like heavy rain, heatwaves, air pollution, traffic congestion, or sudden restrictions can significantly reduce their working hours, leading to <b>20–30% income loss</b>.
</p>

<p>
Existing insurance solutions mainly cover health, accidents, or vehicles, but there is no insurance model that protects gig workers from income loss caused by environmental disruptions.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>
<img src="https://img.icons8.com/color/28/user.png"/>
Persona-Based Scenario
</h2>

<h3>Ravi – Food Delivery Partner</h3>

<ul>
<li><b>Age:</b> 26</li>
<li><b>Platform:</b> Food delivery platforms</li>
<li><b>Daily Earnings:</b> ₹800–₹1000</li>
<li><b>Location:</b> Urban delivery zones</li>
</ul>

<h3>Scenario</h3>

<p>
Ravi starts his shift expecting to complete 15–18 deliveries. Suddenly heavy rain begins in his area. Orders reduce and roads become unsafe, forcing him to stop early. By the end of the week, he loses nearly <b>₹3000–₹4000</b> in income.
</p>

<p>
GigShield ensures Ravi receives automatic compensation when environmental disruptions affect his ability to work.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>
<img src="https://img.icons8.com/color/28/workflow.png"/>
Application Workflow
</h2>

<ol>
<li>Worker registers on the GigShield platform</li>
<li>System generates a Universal Worker Insurance ID</li>
<li>AI models analyze historical weather, pollution, and traffic data</li>
<li>System calculates a weekly premium based on disruption risk</li>
<li>Platform continuously monitors real-time environmental data using APIs</li>
<li>If disruption thresholds are crossed, parametric trigger activates</li>
<li>Worker location and activity verified using GPS</li>
<li>Automatic payout transferred to worker’s bank account or wallet</li>
</ol>

<hr>

<h2>
<img src="https://img.icons8.com/color/28/money.png"/>
Weekly Premium Model
</h2>

<p>
GigShield uses a dynamic weekly premium model aligned with gig workers’ earning cycles.
</p>

<p>Premium calculation considers:</p>

<ul>
<li>Environmental risk level in the worker’s delivery area</li>
<li>Historical disruption frequency</li>
<li>Worker activity patterns</li>
<li>Predicted disruption probability using AI models</li>
</ul>

<p>
Workers pay small weekly premiums, making the insurance affordable and flexible.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>
<img src="https://img.icons8.com/color/28/lightning-bolt.png"/>
Parametric Triggers
</h2>

<p>
Parametric insurance triggers are predefined conditions where payouts automatically activate.
</p>

<ul>
<li>Rainfall exceeding a defined threshold</li>
<li>Air Quality Index (AQI) reaching hazardous levels</li>
<li>Extreme temperature conditions</li>
<li>Severe traffic congestion</li>
<li>Government-imposed movement restrictions</li>
</ul>

<p>
Once these conditions are detected, the system automatically triggers compensation without manual claim filing.
</p>

<h2>
<img src="https://img.icons8.com/color/28/artificial-intelligence.png"/>
AI / ML Integration
</h2>

<h3>Premium Calculation</h3>
<p>
Random Forest and Gradient Boosting models predict disruption risk and calculate fair premiums.
</p>

<h3>Disruption Forecasting</h3>
<p>
LSTM time-series models analyze historical environmental data to forecast high-risk conditions.
</p>

<h3>Fraud Detection</h3>
<p>
Isolation Forest identifies suspicious patterns such as abnormal location data or unusual claim behavior.
</p>

<h3>Risk Zone Mapping</h3>
<p>
AI generates heatmaps highlighting high-risk delivery zones.
</p>
